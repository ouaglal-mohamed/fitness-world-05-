The best 7 Supplements Workout

CREATINE
Creatine causes weight gain because it leads to increased muscle mass in the person who exercises. Without physical activity, there will be weight gain, but no muscle mass.
Branched Chain Amino Acids (BCAAs)
Taken before, during and after strength training, BCAAs can increase muscle mass and limit protein degradation (anticatabolic role). It is therefore a dietary supplement of choice for mass gain.
Fish Oil
The so-called ESSENTIAL FATTY acids, more commonly called omega-3s, have a major role in the protection of the body and the development of the child. The human body cannot manufacture omega-3s: they must therefore be sought in food and supplements
Glutamine
It has an anti-catabolic effect since it limits muscle degradation, optimizes the synthesis of Leucine in fibers and decreases lactic acid. Glutamine also works in muscle development by engulfing them with beneficial substances such as minerals or amino acids.
Whey Protein Powder
Whey protein helps build muscle mass, no more and no less. The amino acids it contains are all essential for the body and help rebuild muscle after exercise.
Beta-Alanine
This supplement has been shown to improve performance in addition to its positive impact on health. By combining with histidine, beta-alanine produces carnosine which is then stored in our muscles, where it works by reducing the build-up of lactic acid.
Carbohydrates
Energy role. The main role of carbohydrates is to provide energy to the cells of the human body (1g of carbohydrates provides 4 calories). When we eat them, they turn more or less quickly into glucose, which is the fuel for certain cells in the body. This is the case with brain cells.
